PLC Aenda

Meeting Called to Order
The Senior Patrol Leader begins the meeting by calling it to order, usually around 6:00 PM. The prayer is said by the Chaplains Aid, and the meeting begins.

Roll Call
The Scribe takes note of everyone that is present, and records it.

Read Previous Meeting Log
The notes from the previous meeting (recorded by the scribe) are read and reviewed.

Patrol Leaders' Reports
Every patrol leader present gives a short report on how his patrol is doing, mostly in terms of advancement.

Old Business
Previous meetings and events are discussed. We discuss how they went, and how we can improve them in the future.

New Business
Upcoming meetings and events are discussed. This includes campouts, meeting agendas, events, and anything else that needs to be planned for or simply brought up.

Planning Ahead
This is a short overview of when the next PLC meeting is. We also note any meetings that will be cancelled or changed. This discussion is often very brief, as it is only an overview.

Scoutmaster's Minute
The scoutmaster is given the full attention of the PLC. This is an opportunity for the scoutmaster to offer any input, on any topic that is important. This can include comments on how to improve our meetings, reminders, or anything else. Because much of the talking is done by the scouts during the PLC, it is important to devote a portion of the meeting to allow the adult leaders offer their input.

Meeting Planning A theme is assigned to each month, such as hiking, public service, and sports. The troop meetings of each month are modelled around these themes, and incorporated into the meeting agenda. This portion of the meeting is used to discuss how to apply the monthly theme to the meeting, through activities, skills instruction, and patrol activities.

Closing The Senior Patrol Leader makes a motion to adjourn, which is seconded by another member of the council. The scribe writes down what time the meeting is adjourned.
