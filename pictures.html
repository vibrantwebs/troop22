Pictures

Here is an extensive photo collection of scouting pictures. Here you can find pictures of campouts, ceremonies, activites, and much more. Each picture has a small caption below it to tell you more about the picture.


Here is a picture of our Troop on its 75th anniversary, taken in front of Saint Francis Church. During Polar Bear Camporee in December, our Troop sleeps and stores our gear in shelters like these every year. Shelters are constructed by the scouts using only tarps, logs, twine, and straw for bedding. This picture was taken in our meeting room during aviation merit badge class. The scouts in this picture are flying a plane on a flight simulator.
This picture shows how to properly keep the scout uniform, hung up and wrinkle free at all times, even on a campout. This picture, taken during Polar Bear Camporee, shows several scouts working together in a lashing activity. Lashing is the method of using rope or twine to bind two or more logs together. Another Polar Bear Camporee activity. This activity challenged the scouts to create a fire under the pressure of time.
The Troop 22 Court of Honor is held twice a year. It is an awards ceremony that recognizes scouts for merit badges, rank advancements, and other awards. As you can see, Troop 22 did a good job this time... This picture was taken during the Polar Bear Camporee of Troop 22's campsite. The shelter in the background is used for sleeping and storing gear. The Chili/Chicken Noodle Soup Supper is an anual event held at Saint Francis School. The proceeds from the supper benefit the Troop. The scouts in this picture from way back are enjoying the pleasure of scraping and washing the dishes. Fun!
This photo was taken at a composites seminar and trade show in Saint Louis. Several scouts from Troop 22 attended the event to complete some requirements for the composites merit badge. (A composite is a combination of two substances that, when combined, create a new substance stronger than the two base substances.) The Lincoln Parade was held during the Lincoln Pilgrimage Camporee in Springfield. The parade began at Lincoln's Tomb and totalled about 2 miles. This is a photo of the scouts at the opening ceremony before the start of the parade. The Lincoln Pilgrimage Camporee was a weekend-long event including a parade and a 20 mile hike. This photo was taken at the end of the trail after several hours of walking.
The 2005 National Jamboree is a week-long camping event held at Fort A.P.Hill, Virginia. The Jamboree had about 45000 scouts from all over the United States and abroad. The contingent Jamboree Troop held two "practice" campouts, at Saukenauk Scout Reservation and Mount Pleasant, Iowa, to prepare for the Jamboree. This photo was taken during one of these "shakedown" campouts. The Lincoln Pilgrimage Camporee was a weekend-long event including a parade and a 20 mile hike. The hike had 3 rest stops along the way. Each rest station had food, drinks, and bathrooms. This is the 3rd and final rest stop. Everyone looks tired. This picture was taken during a 15 miles bike hike in Grafton, Illinois. The bike hike was part of a weekend campout, which also included a visit to Raging Rivers Waterpark.
Saint Francis Hills, owned by Saint Fracis Parish, is used by several groups for campouts, barbecues, and other events. The Hills have several campsites, a brige, a large activity field, and a shelter house. The scouts of Troop 22 are the designated "caretakers" of the property. At many campouts, especially summer camp, scouts put their lashing skills to the test constructing large towers and camp gadgets for competition and fun. This scout is "testing" one of the towers. The aviation merit badge teaches scouts the basics of how planes work, how to fly one, how to plot flights, and much more. This class was held in the library.
The Order of the Arrow, or the OA, is scouting's national honor society. Scouts are elected and inducted into the order in an impressive ceremony at Saukenauk Scout Reservation during Summer Camp. This picture shows a typical member of the ceremony team. Hiking is a favorite activity among the Troop 22 scouts. The scout motto is be prepared. As you can see, we were well prepared to hike, rain or shine. The Boy Scouts participate an annual food drive to re-stock the shelves of the local food pantries, which benefit the poor. The effort includes placing bags on Quincy resident's doors and returning the next week to collect the food. The collected food is then unloaded and organized, placed in baskets, and stocked in the pantry.
The aviation merit badge teaches scouts the basics of how planes work, how to fly one, how to plot flights, and much more. The final requirement of the merit badge class included a plane ride. During the flight, the participants found and pointed out several locations including Quincy, Hannibal, Mendon, Saukenauk Scout Reservation, and many other places.
